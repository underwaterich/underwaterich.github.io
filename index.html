Honours Research Project

In 2024/25, I designed a research project with Deakin University, Earthcare St Kilda, and the Department of Energy, Environment and Climate Action to try to compare methodologies to track abundance and distribution of invasive Northern Pacific Sea Stars in Port Phillip Bay. After thoroughly reviewing the avaiulable literature, I decided to compare drop camera with eDNA surveillance. I selected 3 key sites within Port Phillip Bay where Northern Pacific Sea Stars are known to be endemic. At each site, I ran 5 transects with 10 drop points along each transect. collecting both a water sample and a benthic video sample at each point, ending up with 150 of each sample. After filtering the water samples, extracting eDNA and comparing them to synthetic species specific DNA using qPCR, I ultimately found that these two methods appear to detect different things and are best considered as complementary methods that serve different purposes depending on the question you would like to answer.
