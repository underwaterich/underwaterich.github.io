Offered PhD Position in Cairns

üìÖ 28 November 2025 üìç Melbourne, Victoria üë• Lana Austin, Dianne Gleeson (optional)

"PhDs ruin lives" Thats the lesson from one of the most inspiring people I have ever met Lana Austin. I saw her PhD threaten her to her very core. Lana is an extreme high-achieving, ecologist. I watched, I listened, I learned. I resolved I would not do a PhD.I'm too old. I'm not smart enough. I'm too tired. I don't code well enough. I'm not fit. I have no money. What about Smith?

Surprise! The perfect PhD presented itself - I did not go looking, but there it was being sent to me by a colleague. I had a quick read. and I knew I had no choice but to apply. I mean, I can always deceide later right? No, I don't want to do a PhD, I want to do THIS PhD. This is me to a T. My PhD follows naturally from my honours research project using eDNA to detect invasive species, but this time we are moving from the sea into the air! But I want adventures!! I don't want to be stuck. Well, THIS PhD requires me to move to Cairns because it involves extensive travel around the top end of Australia. Wait, what if I can go to Cape York with this? I could travel all around Darwin, who knows where this will lead? I could address my big career goal of woking with Indigenous Australians. I will develop my skills in communicating complex concepts to a wide audience. I will develop my molecular skills to answer important questions and I will be doing real research which will benefit Australias biosecurity, something I am very passionate about. It's not WILL I do this? it's how could I not? OMG guys. I interviewed for this thing and guess what?

The interview was incredible. I interveiwed better than I have before. I was all over it and everyone knew that. I know the science and In understand the space. They offered me this very competitive PhD same day. They loved me! and I loved them they seem like really thoughtful, considerate, compassionate and caring people and I would be thrilled to have them as my supervisors.
